# The InstructGPT PPO Objective: KL and Pretraining Mix

You understand the core objective: we want to maximize the expected reward generated by our policy $\pi_\theta(y \mid x)$ as evaluated by the Reward Model $r(x, y)$.

However, optimizing *only* for $r(x, y)$ leads to catastrophic **Reward Hacking**. The policy will start outputting gibberish or extremely repetitive phrases that exploit blind spots in the frozen Reward Model to achieve infinite scores.

To prevent this, InstructGPT introduces two penalties in their final PPO objective function:

$$
\text{objective}(\phi) = E_{(x, y) \sim D_{\pi_\phi^{RL}}} \left[ r_{\theta}(x, y) - \beta \log \left( \frac{\pi_\phi^{RL}(y \mid x)}{\pi^{SFT}(y \mid x)} \right) \right] + \gamma E_{x \sim D_{pretrain}} [\log(\pi_\phi^{RL}(x))]
$$

Let's break down the two components you asked about.

### 1. The Per-Token KL Penalty: $-\beta \log \left( \frac{\pi_\phi^{RL}(y \mid x)}{\pi^{SFT}(y \mid x)} \right)$

This term is a dynamic penalty applied *during* the generation of the response.
* $\pi_\phi^{RL}$ is your active PPO model.
* $\pi^{SFT}$ is the original, frozen supervised fine-tuned model (your starting point/reference).

**The Intuition:**
For every single token the PPO model generates, we compare its probability distribution over the vocabulary to what the frozen SFT model would have predicted. 
If the PPO model is drifting too far away from the natural language distribution it learned during SFT (e.g., trying to generate weird tokens just to hack the RM), the ratio $\pi^{RL} / \pi^{SFT}$ becomes large.

Taking the log of this ratio gives us the Kullback-Leibler (KL) Divergence. We subtract this KL value directly from the Reward Model's scalar score $r(x,y)$.

* **Effect:** The model is incentivized to maximize the reward *while staying statistically as close as possible* to the base SFT model's output distribution. The $\beta$ coefficient controls how strictly we leash the PPO model to the SFT model.

### 2. The Pretraining Mixing Term (PTX): $+\gamma E_{x \sim D_{pretrain}} [\log(\pi_\phi^{RL}(x))]$

This term is the "Alignment Tax" mitigation.

**The Intuition:**
When you fine-tune a model extensively with RLHF to be a perfectly polite, harmless assistant, it often undergoes "catastrophic forgetting." It loses its ability to perform generic NLP tasks like coding, translation, or trivia that it learned during its initial pretraining base-model phase. It becomes polite, but dumb.

* **Effect:** InstructGPT mixes in raw, unsupervised pretraining data updates *during* the PPO phase. This term is literally just the standard next-token prediction cross-entropy loss we discussed earlier, applied to normal web text. 
* The $\gamma$ coefficient controls how heavily we weight this term. If we include it, we force the model to maintain its general knowledge while simultaneously learning to align to human preferences on the conversational prompts.
